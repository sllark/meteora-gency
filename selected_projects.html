A few summers after graduate school, I was present with the Hope Diamond as it was removed from its vault and transferred to a darkened room. Tradition dictates âWhoever owns the Koh-i-Noor rules the world.â Legend dates the origin of the Koh-i-Noor back 5000 years; fact dates it to perhaps the year 1300. Its first documented account dates to the early sixteenth century and the memoirs of Babur, the first Mogul emperor. Since that time it has resided in India, Persia, Afghanistan, what is now Pakistan and England. The British annexed the Punjab in the Treaty of Lahore in 1849, and thus began the diamond's fascinating association with the British Crown.

Specifics of the treaty dictated that the Koh-i-Noor be surrendered to Queen Victoria. The jewel was soon after exhibited at the historic Crystal Palace Exhibition of 1851. The diamond was then recut, reduced from its Mogul Indian form of approximately 186.2 carats to a standard brilliant oval cut of 105.602 carats. The Koh-i-Noor is presently set in Queen Elizabeth the Queen Motherâs Crown of 1937 and is displayed in the Jewel House at the Tower of London.
